ARMIN(18 B.C. - A.D. 21)

Germanic tribal leader. Armin (or Arminius) united several Germanic tribes and defeated three Roman legions in the battle of the Teutoburg forest ( Black Forest ) in A.D. 9, which led to the loss of the province "Germania" for the Roman empire.

Armin was also involved in tribal warfare with the Marcomanni. Although he was murdered by his own people Armin became in subsequent centuries a German national hero.

Alamanni

The Alamanni were originally composed of fragments of several Germanic peoples; they were first mentioned in AD 213 in connection with a Roman attack.

In the 5th century they moved into Alsace and northern Switzerland. In 496 they were conquered by Clovis and incorpoarted into his Frankish dominions.

The Franks

The Franks were a Germanic people that invaded the Roman Empire in the 5th century AD.

The Franks advanced into Germany and Gaul (France), and they established the most powerful Christian kingdom of early medieval western Europe.

Clovis (ruled 481-511) extended his authority over the other Franks and conquered all of Gaul. His successors of the Merovingian dynasty (named after Clovis' grandfather Merovich) ruled until displaced by the Carlongian family in the 8th century.

ERMANERIC (c.305 - c.376)

King of the Ostrogoths. Ermaneric established a vast Ostrogothic kingdom from the Baltic to the Black Sea . He struggled successfully with the Romans.

Ermaneric committed suicide because he despaired of success-fully resisting the Huns, who invaded his territories in the 370s.

His kingdom was destroyed and his people became subject to the Huns for 75 years.

Theodoric (455 - 526)

As conqueror of Italy Theodoric the Great was the second Barbarian to rule as king in Italy after the fall of the Roman empire.

Theodoric was a Ostrogothic chieftain who led his people into Bulgaria, and threatened to move to Constantinople. The emperor of the Eastern Roman empire hastily appointed Theodoric king of Italy in order to divert the Ostrogoths from invading the Eastern empire.

Italy at that time was ruled by Odoacer. Odoacer was the Germanic commander of the Roman army who had deposed the last Roman emperor in the West.

Theodoric invaded Italy in 489, forcing Odoacer to take refuge in Ravenna. The city surrendered in 493; Theodoric invited Odoacer to a banquet and there killed him.

ARDASHIR I (180 - 241)

Ardashir overthrew the Parthian monarchy and establ. the neo-Persian empire of the Sassanides.

Ardashir was a descendant of Sassan. His father, Papak, was a vassal of the chief petty king in Persis, Gochihr. Papak killed Gochihr and took the title of king and Ardashir succeeded him to the throne in 208.

Ardashir defeated the Parthian army and entered the capital of Ctesiphon where he was crowned with the imperial title 'king of kings'.

Sassaninian Empire

Sassaninian Empire was called neo-Persian because of his appeal to Persia's past. One important link to the past was the recall of the ancient religion of Zoroastrianism, which became the new state religion.

Monastic Movement in Egypt.

Antonius is the founder of the monastic movement in Egypt.

He began to practice an ascetic life at the age of 20. He took to heart the saying of Jesus in Matthew: 'sell what you possess and give to the poor.' He therefore preached simplicity and the practise of poverty. Antonius withdrew for absolute solitude to a mountain by the Nile. Around 305 he emerged from his retreat to organize the monastic life of the hermits who imitated him.
